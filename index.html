The Problem

You're guessing. Stop guessing.

Every retail decision without data is a bet. Where to place displays. When to schedule staff. Which aisle gets traffic. You're reading tea leaves when you could be reading foot traffic.

"We moved the endcap but can't tell if it worked."

Without traffic data, you can't measure the impact of merchandising changes. Was that display a hit, or did everyone walk past it?

"I think we're overstaffed in the morning."

You feel it, but can't prove it. Scheduling by gut means wasted payroll during slow hours and customers waiting during peaks.

"Our peak hours feel busy, but are they converting?"

Door counters tell you how many entered. They don't tell you where people went, how long they stayed, or which aisles are dead zones.

Privacy First

Movement, not faces.

Your customers care about privacy. So do we. Bluffline watches how people move — not who they are. No facial recognition. No microphones. No way to identify anyone.

No facial recognition

The system tracks body movement — not faces. It literally cannot tell who someone is. That's not a setting you toggle. It's how it works.

No microphones

There's no audio recording. Not disabled — not installed. The system only sees movement.

Your data stays in your store

Everything runs on the device mounted to your ceiling. Your video never gets uploaded anywhere. You access your dashboard through a secure connection — but the raw footage stays put.

Reliable

If your internet drops, the system keeps collecting data. You just won't be able to check your dashboard remotely until it comes back. Nothing is lost.
